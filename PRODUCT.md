# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Andersonville and north side Chicago locals, cocktail lovers, and LGBTQ+-friendly neighborhood regulars deciding where to spend a slow evening. They arrive on a phone or laptop, often at night, checking the menu, hours, and vibe before walking over or emailing a reservation. Many already know Wil and Charlie from the neighborhood's martini-bar tradition.

## Product Purpose

A single-page marketing site for Wil's Martini Lounge (1478 W Catalpa Ave, Chicago). It exists to convey the room's atmosphere (dark, art-forward, intimate), surface the full drink menu with prices, introduce owners Wil Mcerlean and Charlie Bonanno, and convert visits into reservations (mailto), directions, and Instagram follows. Success is a guest who feels the vibe before they arrive.

## Brand Personality

"Classy. Sassy. Definitely bad assy." Warm, welcoming, a little cheeky, deeply neighborhood. Old-school martini glamour (pressed tin, copper light, tufted leather) with a playful modern wink. Come as a regular, leave as family.

## Anti-references

- Generic nightclub sites: neon gradients, bottle-service energy, aggressive parallax.
- Corporate restaurant-group templates: sterile photography grids, "Our Story" boilerplate.
- Minimal all-white cocktail-bar portfolios; Wil's is dark, plush, and personal.

## Design Principles

1. The room is the hero: video, photography, and the mural carry atmosphere; UI stays quiet around them.
2. Personal over polished: Wil and Charlie's faces and voice appear throughout; captions sound like them.
3. Candlelight contrast: near-black grounds, emerald depth, amber/brass accents used sparingly and warmly.
4. Slow-night motion: reveals and drifts are gentle and exponential, never bouncy or busy.
5. Everything converts to a visit: menu, hours, directions, and reserve are never more than one scroll away.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect prefers-reduced-motion everywhere (reveals, marquee, parallax disabled). Maintain readable contrast of cream text on dark grounds. Keyboard-visible focus states on nav and buttons. Videos are decorative, muted, and aria-hidden with poster fallbacks.
